What? When? Where? How?

Examples:
How often do you experience X?
How severe does X get?
What is the impact of experiencing X when you’re Y?
What does X stop you from doing?

Probing questions help you to get more specific information from clients.

Examples:

Is there a particular time of day X happens?
Can you tell me more about X?

Clarifying questions can be used to gather more detail.

Encouraging Questions

Examples:

How would it feel to explore X a little more deeply?
How would it feel to go back and speak to your mother about that?

Encouraging questions are used when you’re dealing with emotional aspects of a client’s life.

Examples:

And when you feel X, how do you respond?
When you think about X, what happens?

Linking questions are used to help make connections.
