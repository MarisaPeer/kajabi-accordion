We are all influenced in some way by our culture and community. Things like religion, family, and education affect our values and the way we see the world.
However, some of these values can directly oppose our own values and limit us from living an authentic life.
An example of this would be if someone told you that being a lawyer means you are more successful than if you were a musician and, as a result, you decide not to pursue a career in music.
It is natural to have a set of values derived from your culture and community. However, if these values stop you from living your life authentically that is a limitation.
As you can see, limiting beliefs come from the places that are most personal to us, which is why they are powerful.
The good news is that you can empower yourself to overcome limiting beliefs. To do this, you must first identify what your limiting beliefs are.
